✨ About Me

I’ve always been drawn to the delicate details that others might overlook. I love reading about history, listening to classical music, and watching ballet—art forms I don’t practice myself, but which remind me of life’s hidden intricacies. Beneath the surface of their seeming rigidity lies a quiet complexity, a rhythm of precision and expression. That same mindset—of unfolding layers to find beauty and meaning—shapes how I approach my work.

I’m a PhD candidate in Statistics and Data Science at the University of Central Florida, where I work at the intersection of deep learning, statistical modeling, and complex systems. My research spans natural language processing, generative models, and applied machine learning across healthcare, genomics, and finance.

With 8+ years of experience, I’ve built tools that analyze gene expression, predict risk in mortgage lending, and discover therapeutic peptides. I develop and apply advanced methods in neural networks, transformers, diffusion models, and geometric deep learning. My foundation in optimization—covering gradient-based methods, convex/non-convex programming, and integer formulations—guides how I design and tune models for complex data.

At heart, I’m a data scientist who loves asking questions. Whether it’s detecting outliers, modeling survival data, or exploring patterns in text or molecules, I use statistics as a lens to uncover structure in noise and trace signals back to their source. I believe analytical thinking is a transferable craft—rooted in mathematics but shaped by curiosity and care.

Currently, I work under the guidance of Dr. Alexandros Mantzaris and contribute to projects like TextSpace.jl. My goal is to bridge research and application—to build models that not only perform but help us understand the systems they represent.
