✨ About Me

I’m a Ph.D. candidate in Statistics and Data Science at the University of Central Florida, advised by Dr. Alexandros Mantzaris. My research focuses on the intersection of geometric deep learning and computational biology, with applications spanning natural language processing, drug discovery, and public health.

My scientific journey began in Iran, where I studied protein topology and molecular dynamics to identify functional binding sites. I used machine learning models and public compound libraries to predict ligand–protein interactions, guiding the design of targeted small molecules.

In 2018, I joined the Omics Hub at the University of South Florida, where I contributed to a team that developed R Shiny dashboards for microbial profiling (16S, metagenomics) used by the Florida Department of Health. I also helped build a power calculator for microbiome studies using PERMANOVA effect size estimation and supported the delivery of hands-on RNA-seq and microbiome analysis workshops. Additionally, I worked on AI models using RNNs and CNNs to identify epigenetic markers of drug resistance in Plasmodium falciparum, showing how noncoding variants affect chromatin accessibility and artemisinin resistance. Through integrative analysis of population genomics and transcriptomics, we identified resistance pathways and candidate genes using evolutionary models such as iSAFE.

For my Ph.D., I expanded into advanced statistical modeling, combining frequentist and Bayesian inference with deep learning architectures including transformers, graph neural networks, and diffusion models. I’ve applied these tools to survival analysis in heart failure patients, consumer behavior modeling in mortgage lending data, and now, two core thesis projects: complex-valued word embeddings that better capture contextual semantics and opposition, and diffusion-based peptide generation targeting antibiotic-resistant bacteria.

Fluent in Python, R, and Julia, I build scalable machine learning pipelines and work across cloud platforms and high-performance computing environments to tackle data-intensive problems.

Outside research, I’m drawn to intricate systems—whether unraveling a Mahler symphony, tracing historical cause-and-effect, or studying the structure of a long-form novel. This same mindset drives my work: layered analysis to transform noise into insight.
